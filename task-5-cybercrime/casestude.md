Case Study: The Anatomy of a UPI Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has revolutionized digital payments in India, but it has also become a primary target for cybercriminals. One of the most prevalent threats is the "Collect Request Scam."

The Scenario:
A victim lists an item for sale on an online classifieds platform. A fraudster posing as a buyer initiates contact, expressing immediate interest. To build trust, the fraudster often sends a fake ID or a screenshot of a legitimate-looking company.

Step-by-Step Execution:

1.The Approach: The fraudster tells the victim, "I have sent the payment; please check your UPI app to receive the money."

2.The Manipulation: Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a "Collect Request" (a payment request) via the UPI app.

3.The Psychological Trap: The victim, seeing a notification that looks like a transaction, clicks the link and is prompted to enter their UPI PIN. The fraudster rushes the victim, claiming the "transaction window is closing."

4.The Consequence: By entering the PIN, the victim unwittingly authorizes a debit from their account to the fraudster’s account. The funds are instantly transferred, and the fraudster immediately blocks the victim.

Impact:
Beyond the immediate financial loss, victims often experience a loss of trust in digital platforms. This highlights that cybercrime is less about "hacking" software and more about "hacking" human psychology through urgency and misinformation.
