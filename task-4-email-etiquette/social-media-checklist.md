Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist


✅ Do's (Best Practices)

1.Audit Your Privacy Settings: Periodically check who can see your posts. Platforms change their terms often; don't assume your "private" profile stayed that way.

2.Engage Authentically: Social media is a two-way street. Reply to comments, ask questions, and support others’ content. It beats shouting into the void.

3.Think Before You Post: Use the T.H.I.N.K. acronym: Is it True, Helpful, Inspiring, Necessary, or Kind?

4.Give Credit Where It’s Due: If you’re sharing someone else’s photo, quote, or idea, tag them. It builds community and avoids "content theft" labels.

5.Keep it Secure: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). Losing an account to a hack is a massive headache you can easily avoid.

❌ Don'ts (Avoid These)

1.Don’t Over-Post: Quality always wins over quantity. Posting ten times a day usually leads to "unfollows" rather than increased engagement.

2.Don’t Feed the Trolls: Getting into a heated argument with a stranger rarely ends well. It’s okay to use the "Block" or "Mute" button for your mental peace.

3.Don’t Post Your Location in Real-Time: For safety reasons, wait until you’ve left a venue or returned from vacation before posting those "wish you were here" photos.

4.Don’t Over-Automate: Direct messages (DMs) that feel like robotic sales pitches are the quickest way to lose credibility. Keep the "social" in social media.

5.Don’t Overshare Personal Details: Avoid posting your home address, phone number, or photos of your boarding pass (the barcodes can be scanned for personal data!).
